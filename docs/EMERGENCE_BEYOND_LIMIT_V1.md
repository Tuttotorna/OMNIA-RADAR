# OMNIA-RADAR · Emergence Beyond Limit v1

## Status

PASS (controlled scenario)

---

## Objective

Test whether OMNIA-RADAR can distinguish:

- pure random noise  
- non-semantic structured sequences  

without relying on semantic interpretation.

---

## Definition

This test does NOT evaluate:

- meaning
- usefulness
- interpretability

It evaluates only:

```text
presence of internal structure


---

Dataset

Two groups:

A — Noise

Random sequences over a fixed alphabet.

Properties:

high entropy

low compressibility

low autocorrelation



---

B — Structure

Sequences generated by a deterministic local rule.

Properties:

low entropy

high compressibility

strong internal dependency


No semantic meaning.


---

Metrics

OMNIA-RADAR score is based on:

compression ratio

entropy

n-gram entropy

autocorrelation


Interpretation:

noise → high entropy, low structure
structure → lower entropy, higher dependency


---

Results

mean noise score:   0.079520
mean struct score:  3.357377

separation:         +3.277857

noise correctly low:    50/50
struct correctly high:  50/50


---

Interpretation

Clear separation between:

random noise
vs
non-semantic structured sequences

This validates:

structure != semantic meaning

OMNIA-RADAR detects structure even when no human meaning is present.


---

Limitations

This is a strong-structure scenario:

compressibility is extreme

autocorrelation is maximal


Therefore:

this test does NOT validate detection of weak or subtle structures


---

Conclusion

OMNIA-RADAR v1 demonstrates:

ability to detect strong non-semantic structure

It does NOT yet demonstrate:

detection of weak or hidden structure beyond apparent limits


---

Next Step

Emergence Beyond Limit v2 — weak hidden structure

Goal:

test borderline cases where structure is partially masked by noise
