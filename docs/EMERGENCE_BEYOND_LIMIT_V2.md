# OMNIA-RADAR · Emergence Beyond Limit v2

## Status

PASS (weak structure scenario)

---

## Objective

Test whether OMNIA-RADAR can detect:

- pure random noise  
- weak hidden structure  
- strong non-semantic structure  

without relying on semantic interpretation.

---

## Key Question

```text
Can structure exist without meaning and still be detectable?


---

Dataset

Three groups:

A — Noise

Random sequences.

Properties:

high entropy

low compressibility

low autocorrelation



---

B — Weak Structure

Mostly random sequences with:

sparse recurring patterns

partial local dependencies

corruption/noise mixed with structure


Properties:

close to noise
but not fully random


---

C — Strong Structure

Deterministic sequences generated by a rule.

Properties:

low entropy

high compressibility

strong dependency

no semantic meaning



---

Metrics

OMNIA-RADAR score combines:

compression ratio

entropy

n-gram entropy

autocorrelation

repeated n-gram excess


Interpretation:

higher score → more internal structure
lower score → closer to random noise


---

Results

mean noise score:        0.744403
mean weak struct score:  0.972549
mean strong struct score:8.446649

noise → weak separation: 0.228146
weak → strong separation:7.474100

ordering pass: True

noise correctly low:     49/50
weak above noise:        46/50
weak below strong:       50/50
strong correctly high:   50/50


---

Interpretation

The system shows a graded response:

noise < weak structure < strong structure

This is critical.

It means OMNIA-RADAR is not binary.

It does not just detect:

structure vs no structure

It detects:

degree of structure


---

Core Result

OMNIA-RADAR detects non-semantic structure even when it is weak and partially hidden.


---

Conceptual Implication

structure != meaning

Even when:

structure is sparse

signal is noisy

no interpretation exists


it can still be measured.


---

Limitations

This remains a synthetic test:

controlled generation

known structure injection

simplified alphabet


Therefore:

this does NOT yet prove real-world detection capability


---

Conclusion

OMNIA-RADAR v2 demonstrates:

detection of structural gradient

Specifically:

noise → weak structure → strong structure

This supports the hypothesis:

post-limit regions may still contain measurable structure
